Role of Income and Institutions in Natural Disaster Deaths

Ninety percent of the disaster victims worldwide live in developing countries where poverty and population pressures force growing numbers of poor people to live in harm’s way on flood plains, in earthquake prone zones and on unstable hillsides. Unsafe buildings compound the risks. The vulnerability of those living in risk prone areas is perhaps the single most important cause of disaster casualties and damageKofi Annan, UN Secretary General, 1999

Over the past few decades, lot of climatic changes have been witnessed due to the Global Warming phenomenon. As a result, frequency of natural disasters has alarmingly increased. Richer nations with high quality institutions such as high democracy score, good political structure and high civil liberty tend to suffer low natural disaster deaths compared to underdeveloped and developing nations. The following visualisation compiling natural disaster occurrences in 137 nations from 1960 till 2014, proves the above fact.
